Progress Thus Far

 

In order to get started with our iridescent modeling, we had to get a better understanding of how we can attempt to render such materials. After our research, we found that a simple way to get started is to apply a thin-film layer. This additional layer causes light waves that are emitted after interference to scatter multiple times in between the original material and the thin-film which creates a colorful, iridescent effect.

 

We first focused on incorporating the optical path difference (difference in distance between the two light paths with respect to the outgoing wave) and the change in reflection that occurs because of the multiple wavelengths impact. Through our research, we found that we can compute the outgoing reflectance using the average of the waves’ amplitudes.
